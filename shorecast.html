Shorecast

This concept app specializes in weather forecasting for surfers so they can get the relevant data they seek in order to plan their time on the water with ease.

My Process

Overview

I began by creating an initial problem statement and a hypothesis.

Competitive Analysis

I began by researching the marketplace and seeing how other products have attempted to solve a similar problem. I conducted a competitive analysis and created a SWOT profile of Surfline to highlight their strengths and weaknesses.

Survey and Interviews

I conducted a survey on 15 surfers with the goal of better understanding surfing behaviors. This gave me some helpful quantitative data, here are some of the key findings. I also interviewed surfers of different skill levels about their experience as a surfer, what information is important to them when surfing, and how they feel about their source for weather forecasting.

Affinity Mapping

To analyze my findings from my interviews I created affinity maps. To do this, I listened to the interviews again and made notes of all the important comments. Then, I began sorting these notes into different categories. I grouped similar answers together into clusters so that I could easily see where participants agree and disagree.

Task Analysis

With my personas in mind, I created an objective based off of problems uncovered in my research. I then created task flows and user flows to solve these problems.

Site Map

The next step was to think about information architecture. I created a site map and ran a card sort to help organize my site categories.

Wireframes

Once I had teh structural foundation for my app established in my site map, I began sketching out wireframes of the screens. During this phase I as not concerned with the visual details, but focusing on how the user would navigate the screens easily and achieve their goals.

High Fidelity Prototype

I created a version of mid-fidelity wireframes using Balsamiq, then, using Adobe XD, I built my wireframes in grayscale, again focusing on core functionality and navigation, then turned those wireframes into a clickable prototype.

Usability Test and Affinity Mapping

I decided to test my prototype to see how well it worked with real people. I created a test script, then began remote testing through Zoom, recording each session. When I was done I began reviewing my tests and created another affinity map. I grouped participant data into categories of observations, positive quotes, negative quotes, and errors.

Rainbow Spreadsheet

To present the data in an organized way, I created a rainbow spreadsheet. This document shows important findings, where each participant had issues, the frequency of the issues, and possible solutions.

Iteration

I now had some problems with my prototype to address. I came up with possible solutions to these problems and implemented them. Since the problems with navigation and functionality were addressed, I then began upgrading the fidelity of the pages, focusing on how I could best utilize visual design methods to make Shorecast more user friendly and aesthetically appealing.

Peer Review

Once all my screens were more polished with color and images, I asked the Career Foundry Slack community of designers and students to critique my project. I received some fantastic feedback from fresh eyes which was very helpful. I then iterated again by making changes to my designs based on this feedback.

Accessibility

Shorecast is for everyone. I wanted to make sure that users with disabilities would have a positive experience using Shorecast. I took another look through my design specifically thinking about accessibility, and made some improvements that should make the tool more inclusive.

Final Mockups

After rounds of testing and iteration, I am feeling confident in my final mockups. I will organize my files and pass on mockups, style guide, assets, and a summary of my research on to the development team.
